User Guide

Everything Dash does, in plain language.

Dash tracks the team's Reddit karma-farming work across all our accounts. It shows, at a glance, which accounts have already hit this week's comment and post quota — and which still need work. This page walks through every screen. No technical background needed.

You're signed in as {{ roleLabel() }} — this guide shows the parts that apply to you.

The two roles

Who sees what
  • Admin — oversees everyone. Sees every Reddit account across the whole team, adds and manages team members, and sets each person's weekly quotas.
  • Reddit Manager — operates their own set of Reddit accounts. Sees only their own accounts and how they're tracking against this week's quota.
“Reddit Manager” is the on-screen name for the role the team informally calls a shiller. Same role, two names.

Getting started

Logging in and finding your way around

Getting an account

You don't sign yourself up. An admin creates your account and gives you an email and password to log in with.

Logging in

Enter your email and password, then Sign in. Wrong details show “Invalid email or password.” Once you're in, your session stays logged in until you log out.

Finding your way around

  • Left sidebar — the main menu: Dashboard, Accounts, and Activity History. Admins also get Team & Access. Your name, role, and Log out sit at the bottom.
  • Top bar — names the current page. On the Dashboard it also shows This week / Last week buttons that switch which week you're viewing. A week runs Monday to Sunday.
  • On a phone — the sidebar tucks behind a menu (☰) button. Tap to open it.

Your Accounts

Open Accounts to manage which Reddit accounts you track.

  • Add an account — select Add, type the Reddit username (e.g. u/example_user), and confirm. There's no “log in with Reddit” step. If the username doesn't exist you'll see “No Reddit user found”; if you already track it, “already tracking.”
  • Remove an account — select remove and confirm. Dash stops tracking it.

Activity History

Activity History is one combined feed of the most recent comments from all your accounts, newest first, each linking out to Reddit — the quickest way to see everything lately in one place.

Reading one account's comments

Select any account (from your dashboard table) to see just its comments. Use the date range picker to narrow the list to a specific stretch of days — handy for checking a particular week.

Team & Access

Team & Access is where you manage the people on the team.

  • Create a member — give them an email and a password (at least 8 characters). They can log in right away.
  • Edit a member — change their email, set a new password, and set their weekly comment quota and weekly post quota. These per-account targets drive the quota bars everyone sees; new accounts start at 50 comments and 5 posts per week.
  • Delete a member — removes them and the Reddit accounts they tracked. Admins can't be deleted, including yourself.
  • Manage — opens a member's page listing the Reddit accounts they track, each linking to its comments.

Understanding the numbers

What the figures and colours mean
  • Karma — Reddit's score for an account: roughly how much positive attention its posts and comments have earned. Growing it is the whole point — it makes an account read as a genuine, established user. Reddit fuzzes the exact figure, so treat karma as a direction, not an exact count.
  • Quota — the target number of comments (and posts) each account should reach in a week. The green bar fills as an account gets closer, and stops at full once the target is met.
  • The week — always Monday to Sunday. “This week” means since the most recent Monday.
On trackGreen — account healthy and hitting its quota.

ProblemRed — the account is banned, suspended, or couldn't be checked.

Good to know

A few things that trip people up
  • Numbers come live from Reddit, so a page may take a moment to load while Dash checks each account.
  • As a Reddit Manager you only ever see your own accounts. Admins see everyone's.
  • There's no “connect with Reddit” step — you track an account by its public username alone.
  • Comments are tracked today; posts are partly there. You'll see post quota progress, but a full feed of individual posts is still coming.
